  • Vinoin Vincely, Karthik Vishwanath. [Accepted]
    "Exploring light-losses in Integrating Sphere measurements using Monte-Carlo simulations."
    Proceedings of SPIE. (2020)

    • The Integrating Sphere (IS) combined with the Inverse Adding-Doubling algorithm (IAD), is a poplar method of extracting broadband optical properties from turbid media. However, IS measurements face errors in the form of light loss. These arise both due to the finiteness of the physical sample and comparable size of the IS sample port and the illumination beam. Here, these error are quantified using a robust Monte-Carlo (MC) simulation. The project will be presented at the SPIE Optics and Photonics conference, Feb 2020.


  • Vinoin Vincely, Karthik Vishwanath.
    "Extracting broadband optical properties from uniform optical phantoms using an integrating sphere and inverse adding-doubling."
    Proceedings of SPIE Vol. 10486 (2018) Link to article

    • The objective of this experiment is to analyze the accuracy of the Integrating Sphere (IS) and the Inverse-Adding Doubling (IAD) algorithm in extracting broadband optical properties of turbid media. Though there exists highly accurate theoretical models, such as Monte-Carlo Simulations, the IAD proves to be a computationally efficient technique. The project explores theoretical and experimental corrections made to the estiamtion technique to improve its overall accuracy. The project was presented at the SPIE Optics and Photonics conference, Jan 2018.

  • Alex Reigle, Kelly Mason, Jessie Slattery, Scott Lee, Tyler Jamison, Adam Eggert, Vinoin Vincely, Darren Wong, Yuliang Guo, Jeffrey Brock, and Mahmud Khan.
    "Superconducting properties of In doped ZrNi2Ga1-xInx"
    Elsevier: Solid State Communications vol. 291 (2019) Link to article

    • The objective of this project is to understand the effects of In doping on the Superconducting properties of ZrNi2Ga1-xInx Husler alloys. Electrical resistivity and DC magnetization measurements were made which showed that the superconducting transition temperature of the compounds decreased with increasing In concentration. All samples showed paramagnetic ordering above their respective superconducting transition temperature. The observations suggest that for x ~ 0.45 the ZrNi2Ga1-xInx compound might exhibit a quantum phase transition. This paper was done as a requirement for Contemporary Physics Lab (PHY293).
