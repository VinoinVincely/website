Courses

Physics Courses

PHY586 - Advanced Computational Physics

  • Applied 4th order Runge-Kutta methods to solve ODEs
  • Numerical differenciation techniques (central and exponential difference methods)
  • Numerical integration methods (Gauss-Quadrature technique)
  • Root finding techniques (Bisection, Newton-Raphson)
  • Fourier transformations: Wavelet analysis, Applications to analysis of oscillatory and chaotic systems

PHY671 - Advanced Electromagnetism

  • Advanced electromagnetic theory and applications.
  • Multipole fields, Maxwell's field equations, Evanescent waves
  • Reflection and Refraction, Radiating systems, Classical Electron Theory, Spherical waves, Interference phenomena, and Diffraction theory.

PHY537 - Thermodynamics & Introduction to Statistical Mechanics

  • Development of formal thermodynamics including first, second, and third laws.
  • Thermodynamic potentials, Maxwell’s relations, phase transitions, and illustrative applications of thermodynamics.
  • Introduction to kinetic theory approach to behavior of systems not in equilibrium, Boltzmann Equation, and transport processes.
  • Development of statistical mechanics and ensemble approach to equilibrium statistical thermodynamics.

PHY551 - Classical Mechanics

  • Introduction to Euler-Lagrange Equations to solve complex systems.
  • Two-Body central force problems, Coriolis Forces.
  • Hamiltonian Mechanics in multidimensional systems.

PHY421 - Molecular & Cellular Biophysics

  • Introduction to physical phenomena acting on molecular and cellular size scales, including transport properties.
  • Thermodynamics and statistical mechanics of reactions, self-assembly, and fluctuations.
  • Development of physical models for biological systems and phenomena, including cooperative behavior in macromolecules.
  • Enzyme Activity, Molecular motors and machines, Energy Transduction, and Nerve Transmission.

PHY483 - Mathematical Methods in Physics

  • Discusses mathematical methods applicable to classical mechanics, quantum mechanics, and electromagnetism.
  • Emphasizes the use of mathematics to model physical systems and methods of solutions to the differential equations of physics.
  • Fourier Transforms, Taylor Expansions, Power Series, Lorentz Transformations

PHY281/282 - Contemporary Physics I & II

  • Expounding the visions of Newton, Schrodinger, Boltzmann, Maxwell, and Einstein.
  • Quantum mechanics in three dimensions, solid state physics, quantum optics, and particle physics.
  • Enabling tools and techniques from atomic, molecular, condensed matter, and particle physics, as well as advances in nanotechnology, quantum optics, and biophysics.

PHY292 - Electronic Instrumentation

  • Basic electronics principles
  • Theory and application of electronic instrumentation for scientists.
  • Data acquisition and analysis with microcomputers.

PHY293/294 - Contemporary Physics Lab & Electronic Instrumentation Lab

  • Explored principles of scanning tunneling microscope, high-Tc superconductivity, and the ‘standard model’ for particle physics.
  • Laboratory experience in the use of electrical and electronic instruments, application of transducers and data acquisition equipment.
  • Use of computer in analyzing data and interfacing computer with experiments.

Computer Science Courses

CSE/BIO 485 - Bioinformatics Principles

  • Concepts and basic computational techniques for mainstream bioinformatics problems.
  • Transforming biological problems into computable ones and seeking solutions.

CSE/BIO 466 - Bioinformatics Computing Skills

  • Use of BLAST, BioPerl, BioPHP, and MatLab Bioinformatics Toolbox. Emphasis placed on biological database design, implementation, management, and analysis.
  • Design and build dynamic websites for bioinformatics applications using tools such HTML, XML, SQL and PhP.
  • Wrote programs in Perl to implement bioinformatics software applications.

CSE174/271 - Fundamentals, Problem-Solving and Object-oriented Programming

  • Learned the fundamental approaches of programming in Java.
  • Modular programming using sequence, selection, and repetition control structures. Program debugging and testing. Formatted input/output.
  • design and implementation of software using object-oriented programming techniques including inheritance, polymorphism, object persistence, and operator overloading.
